Background

A graphic designer who became
fascinated by web development

I’m a UX Designer based in Atlanta, GA who made the transition into product design after doing graphic design and marketing for small agencies and tech companies. I began to take an interest in coding after continuing to see the developers at the ticketing software company where I worked in Charleston, SC restyle our white-label application to blend in with customers’ brand sites. That initial interest inspired me to take an introductory course in front-end development in my free time, which only furthered my fascination with web design and development. Not long after, I decided to leave my home in Charleston in order to enroll in a full-time User Interface Design bootcamp in Raleigh, NC and haven’t looked back since.

In the time following, I’ve worked as a UX designer for an early-stage startup in Atlanta. Because it was a small, two-person design team, I was able to help with all areas of user experience design from conducting high-level user research to leading ideation sessions all the way through creating wireframes, mockups, and prototypes of new and enhanced features.

As someone who’s inroads to the field of user experience design was a long-standing interest and skillset in graphic design, I would say that I initially approached UX as a visual designer without a strong understanding of what not only makes a design beautiful, but also the right design for users. Since that time, my knowledge of UX principles and practices has grown and I would now say I approach design like any kind of scientific inquiry—by first identifying the important knowns and unknowns in a problem and then asking the right questions in order to make better-informed decisions later in the process.

When I’m not creating wireframes or filling up walls with sticky notes, I like to take my golden retriever on long walks and practice yoga. I’m actually in the process of becoming a certified yoga instructor and hope to start teaching part-time within the next year.
